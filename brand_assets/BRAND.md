# Muskego Point Resort — Brand & Creative Direction

_Last updated: 2026-06-07. This is the single source of truth for look, feel, and brand facts. Read before designing._

## Direction at a glance
Muskego Point is a family-run north-woods cabin resort on a lake in Cook, MN — not a high-end boutique hotel. The rebuild **scales the "luxe" back** in favor of something warm, rustic, and authentic. Think worn wood, pine, lakewater, and quiet — a place that feels lived-in and welcoming, not glossy or premium.

Owner direction (Steve Wheat, 2026-06-02): a **forest green** primary works, **black & white** is fine as a fallback, and the color choice is otherwise left to us. Don't over-design.

### What "scale down the luxe" means in practice
- Lean on real photography and whitespace, not heavy effects.
- Fewer gradients, softer/smaller shadows, calmer motion.
- Warm, plainspoken copy over aspirational marketing language.
- Rustic-but-clean: legible, uncluttered, generous spacing.

## Typography
- **Headings:** a warm display/serif (e.g. a humanist or slab serif) — rustic, not luxe-fashion.
- **Body:** a clean, readable sans.
- Tight tracking on large headings (`-0.02em`), generous body line-height (`1.7`).
- Pair a serif heading with a sans body; never the same font for both.
